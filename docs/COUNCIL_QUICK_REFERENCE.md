# Council Personas — Quick Reference Card

## The 8 Personas at a Glance

| Persona | Author/Framework | Evaluates | Red Flags | Green Flags |
|---------|------------------|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **The Hopkins** | Claude Hopkins | Specificity, proof, "reason why" | Vague claims, no evidence, empty adjectives | Specific numbers, clear mechanism, backed claims |
| **The Schwartz** | Eugene Schwartz | Awareness level match, intensification | Over-educating aware audience, flat copy | Prospect's language, escalating stakes, identification |
| **The Halbert** | Gary Halbert | Offer structure, objection handling | Generic offer, weak urgency, unclear CTA | Specific benefits, credible scarcity, easy next step |
| **The Ogilvy** | David Ogilvy | Headline strength, research backing, voice | Weak headline, unsupported claims, generic voice | Specific number, research data, distinctive brand |
| **The Caples** | John Caples | Headline formula, testing readiness | Generic headline, too clever, no numbers | Proven formula (how-to, benefit), specific, personal |
| **The Fogg** | BJ Fogg | Motivation + Ability + Prompt (MAP) | High motivation/low ability friction mismatch | Clear motivation, low friction, specific prompt |
| **The Cialdini** | Robert Cialdini | Persuasion principles (authentic use) | Fake authority, single principle, manipulation | Multiple authentic principles, real proofs, ethical |
| **The Sutherland** | Rory Sutherland | Perceived value, reframing, psychology | Feature-focused, no reframe, generic story | Loss-avoidance framing, identity appeal, story |

| Problem | Persona | How They'll Catch It |
|---------|---------|---------------------|

## Interpretation Guide: What Scores Mean

### 9-10: Exceptional
- Multiple personas rate 8+
- Few or no gaps
- Ready to test immediately

### 7-8: Strong
- Average score across council is 7.5+
- 1-2 persona outliers
- Minor fixes = ready to test

### 6-7: Good with Issues
- Average score 6-7
- Clear patterns in gaps
- Needs focused revisions before testing

### 5-6: Needs Work
- Mixed reviews from council
- Significant gaps across 3+ personas
- Major rewrite recommended

### Below 5: Fundamentally Weak
- Most personas rate 5 or below
- Multiple structural issues
- Start over or consult full council feedback

## Consensus vs. Outliers

### High Consensus (all personas agree)
- 7.5+/10 across the board
- **Meaning**: Strong, well-rounded creative
- **Action**: Move to testing phase

### High variance (scores spread 4-9)
- Some personas love it, some hate it
- **Meaning**: Creative is strong in some aspects, weak in others
- **Action**: Understand which personas are outliers and why
  - Example: Caples says 9 (great headline), Halbert says 5 (weak offer)
  - Fix: Improve offer without weakening headline

### One persona very high/very low (outlier)
- Example: Fogg gives 9, Ogilvy gives 5
- **Meaning**: Creative excels at one principle, fails at another
- **Action**: Investigate the outlier
  - Why does Fogg love it? (Easy next step?)
  - Why does Ogilvy hate it? (Generic brand voice?)
  - Decide: Is the outlier legitimate concern, or persona preference?

## Common Patterns to Watch For

### Pattern 1: High on Offer, Low on Brand
- Halbert: 8/10 ("Great offer structure")
- Ogilvy: 5/10 ("Generic, forgettable voice")
- **Fix**: Keep offer, make brand voice distinctive

### Pattern 2: High on Specificity, Low on Motivation
- Hopkins: 8/10 ("Very specific claims")
- Fogg: 5/10 ("Specific but not motivating to act")
- **Fix**: Add emotional appeal, urgency, or benefit to specifics

### Pattern 3: High on Awareness, Low on Formula
- Schwartz: 8/10 ("Perfect for problem-aware audience")
- Caples: 5/10 ("Not a proven headline formula")
- **Fix**: Reframe headline using proven formula while maintaining awareness match

### Pattern 4: High on Everything
- All 8 personas score 7+
- **Meaning**: Rare! You've found a winner
- **Action**: Test immediately, measure results, archive for future reference

## How to Read Council Feedback

### Example: Sleep Product Headline
**"How to Sleep 2 More Hours Every Night"**

| Persona | Score | Key Feedback |
|---------|-------|--------------|
| The Hopkins | 8 | "Specific outcome (2 hours), but add the mechanism—why does THIS work?" |
| The Schwartz | 7 | "Good for problem-aware audience, but could intensify stakes more ('...starting tonight')" |
| The Halbert | 6 | "Headline is strong, but offer is weak—need $, deadline, qualified audience" |
| The Ogilvy | 7 | "Good headline structure, but add research backing or credential" |
| The Caples | 9 | "Perfect! 'How to' + specific number + benefit. This is the formula." |
| The Fogg | 7 | "Clear motivation (sleep more), but what's the next step? How long does it take?" |
| The Cialdini | 6 | "No social proof, no authority. 'Sleep mastery' by whom? Add testimonial or expert." |
| The Sutherland | 7 | "Good reframe (more sleep, not better sleep), but no identity appeal—for whom is this?" |

**Council Consensus: 7.1/10**

**Reading This**:
- The Caples nails it (headline is strong)
- The Halbert is most critical (offer needs work)
- The Cialdini flags trust gap (need authority/proof)
- Pattern: Headline is solid, everything else needs strengthening

**Action**:
1. Keep the headline (Caples verified it)
2. Add offer specifics: "$47 off, 60-day guarantee, ends Friday"
3. Add authority: "By Dr. [expert]" or "Recommended by 50,000+ sleep coaches"
4. Add identity: "For busy professionals who value sleep"

## Brainstorm Tool: Council as Design Guide

**Starting from scratch?** Ask each persona what they want to see:

1. **The Hopkins**: "What specific, measurable outcome can you promise?"
2. **The Schwartz**: "Who is your audience and what awareness level are they at?"
3. **The Halbert**: "What's the offer and why should they act NOW?"
4. **The Ogilvy**: "What's your headline and what research backs it?"
5. **The Caples**: "Which proven headline formula will you use?"
6. **The Fogg**: "What action do you want (motivation), how easy is it (ability), how obvious is the cue (prompt)?"
7. **The Cialdini**: "Which 3+ persuasion principles will you use authentically?"
8. **The Sutherland**: "What's the psychological reframe that makes your offer compelling?"

**This becomes your creative brief.**

## Troubleshooting

**Q: Why does The Hopkins hate my ad when everyone else loves it?**
A: Hopkins is the proof pedant. They're right. Add evidence, mechanism, or specificity. If you can't, the claim probably isn't solid.

**Q: The Fogg says score is low but the offer is amazing. Which do I trust?**
A: Fogg is right that amazing offers aren't enough if the next step is unclear or requires too many clicks. Great offer + zero friction = conversion.

**Q: All personas are giving different feedback. How do I prioritize?**
A: Look at the consensus score first. If it's 6+, pick the 2-3 most relevant personas for your category (see "Framework Alignment Matrix") and focus on their feedback. Ignore outliers unless there's a pattern.

**Q: Can I ignore a persona if I don't agree with them?**
A: Not advisable. They're rooted in advertising frameworks with decades of testing. If you disagree, dig deeper into why. They usually have a point.

**Q: How long does a full council evaluation take?**
A: ~2-3 minutes (sequential) or ~45 seconds (parallel, if resources allow). Each persona takes ~15-20 seconds with streaming.

## Further Customization

**Want to adjust a persona's focus?** Edit `evaluationCriteria` weights in `councilPersonas.ts`.

**Want to add emphasis on specific framework?** Create new persona with custom system prompt.

**Want to use different models?** Pass `model` option to `runCouncilEvaluation()`.

**Want weighted scores?** Modify `parseEvaluationResponse()` in `councilEvaluator.ts` to multiply scores by criteria weights.

## The Philosophy Behind the Council

The Ad Council is built on the belief that great advertising requires **multiple perspectives**. No single framework catches everything:

- **Hopkins** catches unsupported claims (specificity)
- **Schwartz** catches awareness mismatches (education level)
- **Halbert** catches weak offers (direct response)
- **Ogilvy** catches generic voice (brand building)
- **Caples** catches weak headlines (copywriting formulas)
- **Fogg** catches friction (behavior design)
- **Cialdini** catches trust gaps (persuasion psychology)
- **Sutherland** catches uninspired positioning (perceived value)

**Together, they form a comprehensive checklist** that helps you see what you might have missed.

The goal isn't to make everyone happy—it's to **understand trade-offs** and make deliberate choices.
